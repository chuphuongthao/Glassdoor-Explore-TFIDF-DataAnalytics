WORK REQUIREMENTS

Data Science

The Contractor Team shall provide technical expertise to identify emerging technologies to support and expand the Customers capabilities. The Contractor Team shall provide technical expertise to identify and implement new or improved methods for aggregating and analyzing disparate datasets. The Contractor Team shall review collected holdings to determine and identify technical information relevant to the Departments mission. The Contractor Team shall engage with application developers to identify potential areas for improvements to existing applications that would improve Departments work flow and data analysis. The Contractor shall conduct open source research utilizing strategies to minimize attribution of queries. The Contractor shall learn and maintain the requisite skills to maximize research methodologies. The Contractor shall maintain a strict need-to-know approach in handling requests.

The Contractor shall identify types of information needed to enable further research as applicable. The Contractor shall provide written products on research results, which can include narratives, presentations and visuals. The Contractor shall provide support to multiple offices, requiring interaction with branch level management on an as-needed basis for technology consultation.

Cyber Security

The Contractor shall liaise with offices outside the Customers department on technical topics.

The Contractor shall document technical discoveries. The Contractor shall provide technical training to other Customer department members as necessary. The Contractor shall participate as a technical expert in interactions with other USG partners. The Contractor shall be responsible for the appropriate handling and storage of technical equipment. The Contractor shall conduct holistic and comprehensive routine audits of electronic systems and networks. The Contractor shall identify instances where Customers personnel are abusing their access to the Customers sensitive data holdings, providing program information to unauthorized personnel, or exhibiting issues requiring further investigation. The Contractor shall perform assigned and self-initiated special audits and monitoring of specific incidents on Customers electronic systems and networks involving the Customers program and personnel. The Contractor shall maintain records of all analysis performed. These analyses shall demonstrate a comprehensive approach. They shall expand beyond the context of the specific issue that surfaced the concern and address any relevant security issue found in the Customers data holdings.

The Contractor shall propose self-initiated tasks to Customer management and initiate upon Customers approval and prioritization. The Contractor shall prepare reports on investigative leads resulting from analytic activities performed under this task. The Contractor shall communicate both verbally and in written format the results of any analysis performed as requested by the Customer. The Contractor shall

provide results that include, but are not limited to investigative reports, research discoveries, recommendations, additional requirements, trends, or other issues. Specific formats will vary by requirement and circumstance. The Contractor will work with Customer to determine format on a case-by-case basis. The Contractor shall, for technical audits, present or document reports in a standard agreed-upon formats as directed by the Customer. The Contractor shall produce a number of deliverables, including but not limited to Routine Technical Audit Schedule and Reports and Special Audit and Monitoring Reports. The Contractor shall prepare a schedule for conducting routine technical audits of Customers systems and networks on a quarterly basis and present it to Customer management for review and approval. The Contractor shall propose a schedule for producing each special technical audit report and present it for Customer management review and approval.
